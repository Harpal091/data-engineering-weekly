A Warehouse Migration Checklist I Actually Trust

The dangerous part of a warehouse migration is not getting data to load once. It is deciding too early that the system is equivalent when the real work of trust, ownership, and consistency is still unresolved.

What I check before calling it done

  • Critical dashboards match the old system closely enough to explain differences.
  • Freshness checks are in place for the new pipeline paths.
  • Ownership is clear for every major upstream dependency.
  • Backfills have been tested, not just happy-path daily loads.
  • Rollback expectations are documented before the cutover window starts.

Why this matters

Migrations fail socially as often as they fail technically. If teams do not know what changed, who owns the new surfaces, or how to interpret differences, the migration becomes a long-running trust issue instead of a clean platform upgrade.
