When dbt Models Become Too Coupled

A dbt project starts to feel heavy long before it becomes technically impossible to maintain. Usually the warning sign is not size by itself. It is how many unrelated questions now require touching the same tightly connected model chain.

What coupling looks like in practice

One change to naming, grain, or business logic creates surprising downstream breakage. Review scope expands. Tests are harder to interpret. Teams become reluctant to clean up old assumptions because the blast radius is no longer clear.

What helps

Clear model intent, stricter boundaries between staging and business logic, and fewer convenience references across domains all make the project easier to change.
